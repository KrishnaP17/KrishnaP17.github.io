Website Evaluations

Good Website: Shade Master

A website that specializes in providing high-quality shading solutions.

  • Contrast: Good - The website utilizes a well-chosen color palette, ensuring text and images stand out against the background, enhancing readability and visual appeal.
  • Repetition: Good - Consistent use of fonts, colors, and styling elements across the pages maintains a cohesive and professional appearance.
  • Alignment: Good - Content is appropriately aligned, creating a visually pleasing layout that guides the user's eyes smoothly through the page.
  • Proximity: Good - Related elements are grouped together, creating a logical flow and making it easy for users to identify and understand the information.

The Shade Master website excels in applying the CRAP principles, contributing to a positive user experience.

Bad Website: Yale School of Art

The official website of the Yale School of Art, presenting information about the school, its programs, and events. However, the website design appears outdated and lacks visual cohesion.

  • Contrast: Poor - The color choices on the website result in low contrast between text and background, making it difficult to read and causing visual strain.
  • Repetition: Poor - Inconsistencies in font styles, sizes, and color usage create a lack of visual harmony and brand consistency across the pages.
  • Alignment: Poor - Elements seem misaligned, causing a disorganized appearance and making it challenging for users to follow a clear visual hierarchy.
  • Proximity: Poor - Related content is not grouped together effectively, leading to confusion and making it harder for users to find relevant information quickly.

The Yale School of Art website falls short in adhering to the CRAP principles, negatively impacting the overall user experience.
