FEATURES - FRONT VIEW

HOVER OVER THE RED DOTS

REAR VIEW MIRROR

The rear view mirror is an essential safety feature that allows the driver to see what's happening behind the car without turning around.

It reflects the view from the rear of the car, helping the driver to monitor traffic conditions and vehicles approaching from behind.

HEADLIGHTS

Description: Headlights are front-facing lights that illuminate the road ahead during low-light conditions or at night.

They improve visibility for the driver and enhance the car's visibility to other road users, contributing to safety and navigation.

SIDE MIRRORS

Side mirrors, also known as wing mirrors, provide visibility to the sides and rear of the car, enhancing situational awareness.

They reflect the view of adjacent lanes and areas not visible directly to the driver, aiding in lane changes, parking, and maneuvering.

FUEL TANK

The fuel tank is where the car stores its fuel, and placing it in the front is a common design for many vehicles.

Placing the fuel tank in the front balances the car's weight distribution, improves stability, and allows for efficient fuel delivery to the engine.

BUMPER/FENDER

The fender or bumper is a protective component that surrounds the front and rear parts of the car, absorbing impact in collisions.

It helps minimize damage to the car's body and internal components by absorbing and dispersing impact forces during minor collisions.

WHEELS

Wheels are the circular components on which the car rests and moves, typically made of metal or alloy.

They support the vehicle's weight, provide traction for movement, and house the tires, which contribute to the car's handling and performance.

HOOD

The hood, also called the bonnet, covers the engine compartment at the front of the car.

It protects the engine and other vital components from external elements, houses sound insulation, and contributes to aerodynamics.
